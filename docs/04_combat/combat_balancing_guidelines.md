# Combat Balancing Guidelines

## Version: 0.1

## Status: Draft

## Owner: Combat Systems

## Last Updated: 2026-03-03

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the design philosophy and balancing guidelines for combat systems.

Unlike subsystem specifications, which describe mechanics and implementation details, this document defines the intended **combat experience and gameplay dynamics**.

Combat in Black Sector is designed around the concept of **submarines in space**:

* long-range engagements
* sensor-driven detection
* ambush and evasion tactics
* strategic positioning
* asymmetric ship capabilities

These guidelines ensure future development maintains the intended tactical feel.

---

# 2. Core Combat Philosophy

Combat should feel tense, deliberate, and information-driven.

Key characteristics:

* ships rarely engage at visual range
* detection and tracking determine combat outcomes
* surprise attacks are possible
* disengagement is often viable

Combat should resemble **naval warfare or submarine engagements** more than traditional dogfighting.

---

# 3. Sensor Warfare

Sensors are the primary driver of combat.

Combat advantages often come from:

* detecting an enemy first
* remaining undetected
* maintaining sensor lock

Detection should be influenced by:

* ship size
* distance
* environmental conditions
* electronic countermeasures

A ship that cannot detect its opponent should not be able to effectively engage it.

---

# 4. Ship Size and Detectability

Ship size strongly influences detectability.

General guideline:

| Ship Class | Detection Profile      |
| ---------- | ---------------------- |
| Small      | Hard to detect         |
| Medium     | Moderate detectability |
| Large      | Easily detected        |

Large ships emit stronger sensor signatures due to:

* reactor output
* propulsion systems
* large hull surface area

Small ships may evade detection more easily.

---

# 5. Environmental Concealment

Environmental features should influence detection and combat.

Examples include:

* asteroid fields
* nebula clouds
* ion storms
* debris fields

Effects may include:

* reduced sensor range
* increased stealth capability
* degraded targeting accuracy

Asteroid fields should provide significant concealment for smaller vessels.

---

# 6. Engagement Distance

Combat should typically occur at **long range**.

Guidelines:

* ships should engage across multiple sectors of distance equivalent
* weapons should operate beyond visual range
* combat should often begin with detection rather than direct confrontation

Long-range combat reinforces strategic positioning.

---

# 7. First Strike Advantage

The first ship to detect and target an opponent should gain an advantage.

Possible advantages:

* initial strike
* missile launch opportunity
* ambush positioning

However, the advantage should not be overwhelming enough to guarantee victory.

---

# 8. Countermeasures

Larger ships rely on countermeasures to survive.

Examples:

* electronic warfare systems
* decoys
* missile interception
* sensor jamming

Countermeasures should allow large ships to survive long enough to respond.

---

# 9. Small Ship Advantages

Small ships should possess tactical advantages despite weaker defenses.

Advantages include:

* stealth
* maneuverability
* environmental concealment
* ambush potential

Small ships should excel in:

* reconnaissance
* harassment
* ambush attacks

---

# 10. Large Ship Advantages

Large ships should excel in:

* durability
* firepower
* sustained combat

Large ships should not be stealthy but should be difficult to destroy.

They often rely on:

* defensive systems
* heat management
* energy reserves

---

# 11. Combat Duration

Combat should not resolve instantly.

Typical engagement durations should allow:

* tactical repositioning
* disengagement attempts
* weapon cooldown decisions

Average engagement time targets:

| Engagement Type | Duration      |
| --------------- | ------------- |
| Small skirmish  | 30–90 seconds |
| Trader ambush   | 20–60 seconds |

Combat should feel meaningful rather than instantaneous.

---

# 13. Escape and Disengagement

Disengagement should remain possible in many encounters.

Ships may escape using:

* warp disengagement
* countermeasures
* environmental concealment

However, escape should carry risk if an enemy maintains sensor lock.

---

# 14. Ambush Gameplay

Ambush tactics should be viable.

Ambush opportunities may arise from:

* asteroid concealment
* stealthy small ships
* sensor blind spots
* economic traffic routes

Ambush gameplay encourages strategic positioning rather than random encounters.

---

# 15. Trader Vulnerability

AI traders should represent viable piracy targets.

However, traders should not be defenseless.

Possible defenses include:

* escort ships
* distress signals
* emergency countermeasures

Trader destruction should generate cargo drops that attract pirates and scavengers.

---

# 16. Risk vs Reward

Combat in dangerous regions should offer higher rewards.

Examples:

| Region Type      | Combat Opportunity |
| ---------------- | ------------------ |
| Core regions     | Low                |
| Frontier regions | Moderate           |
| Black sectors    | High               |

Black sectors should contain the most dangerous and profitable combat encounters.

---

# 17. Monitoring and Balance

Combat telemetry should be monitored to maintain balance.

Important metrics include:

* average combat duration
* ship survival rates
* weapon effectiveness
* piracy frequency

Adjustments should occur gradually to prevent destabilizing combat balance.

---

# 18. Non-Goals

Combat is not intended to resemble:

* arcade dogfighting
* twitch-based shooter mechanics
* instant-kill encounters

Combat should emphasize **strategy, detection, and positioning**.

---

# 19. Future Combat Concepts

Future systems may expand combat depth through:

* cloaking technology
* advanced electronic warfare
* minefields
* drone swarms
* carrier-based fighters

These systems should remain consistent with the submarine-style combat philosophy.
