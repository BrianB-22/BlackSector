# Economy Balancing Guidelines
## Version: 0.1
## Status: Draft
## Owner: Economy Systems

## Last Updated: 2026-03-03

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the guiding principles used to balance the galactic economy.

Unlike subsystem specifications, which describe how systems function technically, this document describes the **intended gameplay outcomes** and **design constraints** for economic systems.

The goal is to maintain a healthy economy that is:

* dynamic
* discoverable
* resistant to exploitation
* supportive of multiple playstyles

These guidelines ensure that future changes remain aligned with the intended economic experience.

---

# 2. Economic Pillars

The galactic economy must support the following pillars.

### Exploration

Players should discover profitable trade opportunities by exploring new regions.

Profitable routes should not be fully obvious from the start of the game.

---

### Regional Specialization

Different regions of space should produce and consume different commodities.

This encourages:

* long-distance trade
* supply chain movement
* economic geography

Example:

* agricultural regions export food
* industrial regions export manufactured goods
* frontier regions export raw materials

---

### Risk vs Reward

Profit potential should increase with risk.

General target model:

| Security Zone   | Expected Profit |
| --------------- | --------------- |
| High Security   | Low             |
| Medium Security | Moderate        |
| Low Security    | High            |
| Black Sector    | Very High       |

Dangerous regions should consistently offer higher economic opportunity.

---

### Market Instability

Markets should shift over time.

Players should periodically need to discover new profitable trade routes.

Economic events and supply changes must prevent long-term stagnation.

---

# 3. Profit Expectations

Typical profit margins should follow predictable ranges.

| Trade Type         | Profit Range |
| ------------------ | ------------ |
| Short local trade  | 5–10%        |
| Regional trade     | 10–20%       |
| Frontier trade     | 20–40%       |
| Rare event trading | 40–80%       |

Profit values significantly above these levels should occur only during rare events.

---

# 4. Commodity Distribution Philosophy

Commodity availability should follow a structured distribution model.

### Essential Goods

Examples:

* food
* fuel
* medical supplies

Characteristics:

* widely available
* low volatility
* moderate demand everywhere

---

### Industrial Goods

Examples:

* refined ore
* machinery
* electronics

Characteristics:

* regionally concentrated production
* higher volatility
* strong trade route drivers

---

### Luxury Goods

Examples:

* luxury artifacts
* entertainment goods

Characteristics:

* rare
* high value
* long-distance transport incentives

---

### Exotic Goods

Examples:

* alien artifacts
* anomaly resources

Characteristics:

* extremely rare
* high volatility
* often tied to exploration

---

# 5. Commodity Volatility Guidelines

Commodity volatility determines price fluctuation sensitivity.

Target volatility ranges:

| Commodity Type   | Volatility |
| ---------------- | ---------- |
| Essential goods  | Low        |
| Industrial goods | Medium     |
| Luxury goods     | High       |
| Exotic goods     | Very High  |

Volatility must remain balanced to avoid runaway price spikes.

---

# 6. Economic Stability Rules

The economic system must avoid the following failure modes.

### Permanent Shortages

No commodity should remain unavailable indefinitely.

Production and consumption must naturally rebalance over time.

---

### Infinite Supply

Ports must never function as infinite commodity sources.

Inventory systems must limit available supply.

---

### Static Markets

Markets should not settle into permanent equilibrium.

Economic events and AI traders must continually alter supply distribution.

---

# 7. AI Trader Balance

AI traders must move a significant portion of economic volume while still leaving opportunity for players.

Recommended target:

| Economic Actor | Trade Volume |
| -------------- | ------------ |
| AI traders     | 60–70%       |
| Players        | 30–40%       |

AI traders should stabilize the economy but not eliminate profitable routes.

---

# 8. Trade Route Philosophy

Profitable trade routes should emerge naturally from:

* regional production differences
* scarcity events
* commodity volatility

Routes should evolve over time.

No single route should remain permanently dominant.

---

# 9. Economic Event Guidelines

Economic events should periodically disrupt markets.

Guidelines:

* events should not occur constantly
* events should not collapse the entire economy
* events should create temporary opportunities

Typical effects include:

* sudden demand spikes
* production shortages
* black market surges

---

# 10. Price Limits

Commodity prices should remain within reasonable bounds.

Suggested limits:

Minimum price:
50% of base value

Maximum price:
500% of base value

Exceeding these bounds risks destabilizing the economy.

---

# 11. Player Impact Goals

Players should be able to influence the economy through:

* large trade shipments
* piracy activity
* regional trade patterns

However, players should not be able to permanently destabilize the economy.

---

# 12. Frontier Economy

Frontier space should behave differently from core regions.

Frontier traits:

* lower supply stability
* higher commodity volatility
* higher risk trade routes
* more frequent economic events

This creates incentives for experienced players to operate in frontier regions.

---

# 13. Black Sector Economy

Black sectors represent the highest risk and highest reward economic zones.

Characteristics:

* contraband markets
* rare commodities
* extreme price volatility
* limited law enforcement

Black sector trading should provide the largest profit potential in the game.

---

# 14. Monitoring and Adjustment

Economic health should be continuously monitored using telemetry.

Key metrics include:

* commodity price variance
* trade route profitability
* port inventory levels
* trader destruction rate
* player trade participation

Balance adjustments should occur gradually to avoid destabilizing the economy.

---

# 15. Non-Goals

The economy is not intended to simulate real-world economics with full realism.

Goals are gameplay-focused rather than academically accurate.

The system prioritizes:

* player discovery
* meaningful decisions
* emergent gameplay

over strict economic realism.

---

# 16. Future Balancing Considerations

Future systems may introduce additional balancing challenges.

Examples include:

* faction-controlled economies
* manufacturing systems
* player-owned infrastructure
* economic warfare
* smuggling networks

Balancing guidelines should evolve as new systems are introduced.
